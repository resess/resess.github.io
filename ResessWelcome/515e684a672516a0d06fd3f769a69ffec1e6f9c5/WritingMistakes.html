    Common Writing Mistakes

    After you have completed writing your document and ready to proofread it, please check for the common mistakes that we often make and correct if any.

    1. Don't forget to insert a whitespace between a word and "("
        Example:
          Share your calendar(Google calendar).
          Share your calendar (Google calendar).

    2. Use Oxford comma style to avoid ambiguity
        Example:
          I love my parents, Eminem and Lady Gaga.
          I love my parents, Eminem, and Lady Gaga.

    3. Do not use passive voice, especially with ambiguous actors.
        Example:
          The work was completed in time.
          I completed the work in time.

    4. Don't use citations as nouns, e.g., [1] shows
    5. Be explicit: "Interesting and unique perspective on problem" --> what is this perspective? "They identified main motivations for developers to do CD, main CD tools used" --> what are these motivations and tools?
    6. Numbers below 10 are spelled out, numbers starting from 10 are given as numbers. Exceptions are when you have a series of numbers, e.g., 10, 4, 6, 12. “out of 22, 18 recorded, 3 no record” is ok, as it is a series; “we interviewed 3 people” is not ok.
    7. Bullet points should be consistent in structure (verbs vs. nouns, punctuation, etc.).
    8. Always include slide numbers.
