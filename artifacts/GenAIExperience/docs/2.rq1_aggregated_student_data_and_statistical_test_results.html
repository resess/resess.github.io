Statistical Tests for Correlation between AI Reliance and Grades

We ran (1) Spearman correlation test to examine the relationship between grades and AI reliance and (2) Mann-Whitney U test to compare grades for high vs. low AI-reliance students, as both tests are appropriate for data of our size and distribution.

We found (1) no statistically significant correlation between students' AI reliance and their grades:

Milestone Spearman’s ρ p-value Conclusion
M3 (Requirements and Design) 0.1257 0.6526 Weak positive correlation, no statistical significance
M4 (Implementation) 0.4267 0.0976 Moderate positive correlation, no statistical significance
M5 (Testing and Code Review) -0.2584 0.3286 Weak negative correlation, no statistical significance

Given the small sample size, we also employed permutation tests to ensure statistical rigor and the results were remarkably consistent between both methods (differences < 0.01).

We found (2) no statistically significant difference in grades between high and low reliance groups:

Milestone Mann-Whitney U p-value Conclusion
M3 (Requirements and Design) 39.50 0.5213 No significant difference between high and low reliance groups
M4 (Implementation) 46.00 0.6243 No significant difference between high and low reliance groups
M5 (Testing and Code Review) 36.50 0.7568 No significant difference between high and low reliance groups

Please note that these statistical results should be interpreted with caution due to the small sample size (n=18).
